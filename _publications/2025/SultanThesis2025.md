#abstract: >-
#  We present Zero-Phase Phasor Fields, a method that improves the depth precision of “seeing around corners” without the usual runtime penalty. Rather than reconstructing hundreds of closely spaced depth slices, we use computational wave cues to refine a coarse reconstruction and locate hidden surfaces between sampled planes. This enables micrometer-scale depth precision while keeping reconstructions fast.
